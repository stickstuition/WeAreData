# Tempero Business Plan

![Tempero logo](Tempero_Logo.png)

## 1. Executive Summary

Tempero is a data-driven planning and insight platform from WeAreData, a UK-based company led by CEO Davide Calvi. It helps teams create value by blending student attendance data, operational insights, and the TIAGO Meta Framework into practical decisions.

Objectives:
- Launch the Tempero MVP and complete a Nova SBE canteen pilot.
- Validate the concept with attendance and service usage data.
- Demonstrate the value of data-driven planning for campus hospitality.
- Use pilot results to shape a broader market offering.

Time horizon:
- 12 months, divided into 4 periods of 3 months each.

## 2. Business Description

Tempero is a warm, human-focused platform that uses data like a good recipe: thoughtfully combined, easy to follow, and built for practical results.

Mission:
- Help teams turn data into decisions that feel smart, simple, and meaningful.

Value proposition:
- Combine attendance and operational data with innovation frameworks.
- Blend smoothly with existing hospitality and canteen systems rather than replacing them.
- Provide clear recommendations for improving service, supply planning, and operations.
- Support teams in delivering measurable value without unnecessary complexity.

## 3. Market Analysis

Target customers:
- Educational institutions managing dining and attendance.
- Hospitality and canteen operators.
- Innovation teams in service businesses.
- Consultants and advisors focused on operational value.

Customer needs:
- Better understanding of attendance patterns and demand.
- Practical tools for planning student-facing operations.
- Support for existing hospitality workflows and operational systems.
- Ability to digitize manual documents, such as invoices, and bring that data into one dashboard.

Pilot context:
- The product will be run and tested by Susanna, who manages the Nova SBE canteen operated by Eurest.
- The pilot is built as a half-semester test (approximately 2 months) to capture student attendance and canteen usage data.
- Data from this pilot will shape Tempero’s broader product fit for campus and hospitality scenarios.
- The pilot will also test document scanning and parsing of manual invoices so the canteen can move paper processes into the dashboard.

Competitive edge:
- Combines Portuguese warmth with UK-based execution.
- Built around the TIAGO Meta Framework and Professor Luis Filipe Lages’s guidance.
- Feels softer and more human than typical enterprise data tools.

## 4. Product / Service Offering

Core product:
- Value creation wheel dashboard.
- Insight pages for trends, ideas, and recommendations.
- Planner and roadmap pages for implementation.
- Document scanning and parsing for manual invoices and other paper records.
- Integration with existing hospitality and canteen systems to blend new insights with current operations.

Premium service offerings:
- Custom reporting and dashboards.
- Team collaboration features.
- Data integration and onboarding consultancy.
- Support for adding paper-based financial and inventory data into the digital workflow.

## 5. Business Model

Revenue streams:
- Subscription revenue from platform access at 39.99€ per user per month.
- One-time onboarding and consulting fees.
- Future premium add-ons and reporting planned for Year 2.

Pricing assumptions:
- Pilot pricing is 39.99€ per user per month.
- Year 1 will be intentionally low-volume as the product proves fit.
- Premium services are reserved for Year 2 once core adoption is established.

Sales channels:
- Direct outreach to Portuguese SMEs.
- Partnerships with business consultants.
- Online marketing and targeted webinars.

## 6. Marketing & Sales Strategy

Positioning:
- "Tempero — dados com sabor e propósito."

Tactics:
- Publish content about innovation, data, and value creation.
- Use pilot case studies to prove impact.
- Run targeted campaigns on LinkedIn and local business networks.

Key metrics:
- Trial-to-paid conversion rate.
- Customer acquisition cost (CAC).
- Monthly recurring revenue (MRR).
- Churn and retention rates.

## 7. Funding Opportunity

Tempero will seek EU grant support to accelerate pilot delivery and product development. The most relevant funding avenue is Horizon Europe, especially SME-oriented calls under the Digital, Industry and Space cluster. This grant interest is aligned with Tempero’s role in digital transformation for hospitality and campus service planning.

Potential grant use:
- support the Nova SBE pilot and canteen integration,
- fund document scanning and invoice parsing development,
- cover early adoption and reporting work with Eurest and academic advisors.

## 10. Milestones

- **Period 1:** Launch MVP, onboard pilot customers, collect initial feedback.
- **Period 2:** Improve retention, secure first paying subscriptions, grow awareness.
- **Period 3:** Expand premium features and consulting packages, increase revenue.
- **Period 4:** Scale customer acquisition, improve margins, prepare Year 2 growth plan.

## 11. Risks and Mitigation

- Risk: slow customer adoption.
  - Mitigation: focus on pilot customers and strong onboarding.
- Risk: high operating cost.
  - Mitigation: keep marketing spend disciplined and prioritize core development.
- Risk: competing solutions.
  - Mitigation: emphasize Tempero’s warmth, clarity, and Portuguese market fit.
